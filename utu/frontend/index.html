02 · Manifesto

Three oaths we keep.

I · CLARITY

Software should reduce confusion, not perform sophistication.

Most products grow heavy because nobody is paid to remove things. We measure quality by what's missing — by the number of clicks the user didn't have to make, by the screens we didn't ship. Restraint is the discipline; clarity is the result.

II · FAIRNESS

A tool is judged by the people who use it under pressure.

Drivers in the rain. Operators on a deadline. The teenager who can't read English. We design for those moments, in those languages — Arabic-first when the work is for Iraq — and we test with the real users, not focus-grouped composites.

III · ENDURANCE

We build to be still standing in five years.

No fashionable frameworks for fashion's sake. No leaky abstractions. We pick boring, well-understood tools, write code our successors can read, and document like we'll be dead tomorrow. The work outlasts the trend that birthed it.

05 · How we work

Four movements.

i

Listen

A two-week paid discovery. We sit with the people who'll use the thing. We read your spreadsheets. We don't bring a deck.

ii

Frame

A written scope: what we're building, what we're not, what it costs, and when. Signed by both sides like a real contract.

iii

Build

Two-week sprints. Demos every Friday. You see the work as it forms; we don't disappear for six months and reappear with a reveal.

iv

Tend

After launch, monthly retainer or a clean handoff to your team — your call. Either way, we leave the codebase someone else could pick up.
